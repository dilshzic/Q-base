2. End-to-End Encryption (E2EE) & Privacy

Q-base utilizes End-to-End Encryption (E2EE) for private chat messages and specific file transfers. This means your communications are encrypted on your device and can only be decrypted by the intended recipients. Q-base, our staff, and our cloud providers do not possess the encryption keys and cannot read the contents of your E2EE communications.

3. User-Generated Content & Moderation

While Q-base cannot proactively scan or read E2EE messages, we are committed to maintaining a safe platform. To enforce our policies against abuse, harassment, illegal content, and Terms violations, we rely on user-initiated reporting.

A. "Report and Forward" Mechanism

If you encounter a message that violates our Terms, you may use the in-app "Report" function. When you report a message:

  • Your device decrypts the reported message along with a small segment of recent surrounding messages to provide necessary context.
  • Your app securely forwards this decrypted, unencrypted copy directly to Q-base's moderation team, along with cryptographic proof of the sender's identity.
  • By reporting a message, you explicitly consent to Q-base accessing the decrypted contents of that specific interaction for moderation purposes.

B. Unencrypted Metadata & Behavioral Moderation

Q-base maintains the right to monitor unencrypted metadata and account behaviors (e.g., message frequency, block rates, unencrypted profile photos, and bios) to proactively identify spam, abuse, or Terms violations.

4. Account Actions & "Take Downs"

Because Q-base cannot remotely delete specific encrypted messages from users' devices without compromising our E2EE architecture, content moderation and "take downs" are performed at the account level.

  • Banning and Suspension: If our moderation team determines that an account has violated these Terms, Q-base reserves the right to ban, suspend, or restrict that account.
  • Client-Side Resolution: When an account is banned, Q-base broadcasts a banned status flag to the network. Your App is programmed to recognize this flag isBanned and may automatically hide chats, block the offending user from your UI, or delete local copies of conversations involving the banned user.
